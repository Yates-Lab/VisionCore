Directly estimating the close-pair density vs the squared-marginal assumption

An implementation note to writeup.md (methods_eyepos_matching)

fem-v1-fovea methods note

2026-06-24

Summary

The eye-position-matched estimator of the main writeup (§4.2, §4.4) reweights every term toward a target eye distribution by importance sampling. The close-pair second moment is sampled from the close-pair density ppairp_{\mathrm{pair}}, and the importance weights need that density. Throughout, the writeup substitutes the squared marginal p̂2\hat p^2 for ppairp_{\mathrm{pair}} — the §A.5 result that close pairs drawn from pp have midpoint density ∝p(e)2\propto p(e)^2, exact in the single-bin Δe→0\Delta e\to 0 limit. In the production trajectory-mode estimator (§4.4) each sample’s eye trajectory is reduced to a geometric-median representative point ρi\rho_i and “close” is judged by the whole-window RMS distance (17), so that identity is only approximate: neither the finite threshold nor the trajectory reduction is guaranteed to leave ppair=p̂2p_{\mathrm{pair}} = \hat p^2.

This note tests the assumption directly. We add a closepair_density={'direct' (default),'squared'} switch to the estimator (estimators.decompose, decompose_trajectory, threaded through pipeline.decompose_session). 'direct' fits a second KDE p̂pair\hat p_{\mathrm{pair}} on the realized close-pair representative-midpoints instead of squaring p̂\hat p. On the same 25 real fixRSVP sessions and the exact §4.5 estimator window we find:

  • The squared marginal is measurably wrong, in a consistent direction. The close-pair variance ratio trcov(ρmid)/trcov(ρ)\operatorname{tr}\operatorname{cov}(\rho_{\mathrm{mid}})/\operatorname{tr}\operatorname{cov}(\rho) has population median 0.420.42 — below the ideal Gaussian value 0.50.5 — with KL(p̂pair∥p̂2)\mathrm{KL}(\hat p_{\mathrm{pair}}\,\|\,\hat p^2) median 0.160.16. Real close pairs are more central than p2p^2 predicts, because real fixation densities are more peaked than Gaussian and the RMS-trajectory match preferentially keeps the stablest central fixations.

  • The headline result is robust. On the reported Direction 1 (full, target pp), the population median 1−α1-\alpha moves only 0.692→0.7090.692\to0.709 (+0.017+0.017; per-cell median shift +0.001+0.001). The naive estimate and the Fano factor are unchanged (ppairp_{\mathrm{pair}} does not enter them). So the §4.5 / Figure-2 / Figure-4 conclusions stand under a directly-estimated close-pair density.

  • Direction 2 and the gap are more sensitive — and 'direct' is the more correct estimator there. central (target p2p^2) moves 0.537→0.4770.537\to0.477 (−0.060-0.060) and the full-vs-central gap widens 0.119→0.1830.119\to0.183. This is expected: ppairp_{\mathrm{pair}} enters central through the per-sample reweight, and 'direct' makes central genuinely consistent over the true close-pair distribution rather than the idealized p2p^2.

On the strength of this comparison 'direct' is now the estimator default (2026-06-24), and the main writeup reports it throughout (§4.5). 'squared' remains available and reproduces the pre-change weights bit-for-bit; it is the natural choice for the closed-form synthetic validation, where p_pair = p^2 holds exactly.

1. Where the p2p^2 assumption enters

The matched estimator (§4.2) reweights toward a target qq by importance sampling. A single trial drawn from pp contributes weight q(e)/p(e)q(e)/p(e) to the mean / CtotalC_{\mathrm{total}} / CpsthC_{\mathrm{psth}}; a close pair, drawn from ppairp_{\mathrm{pair}}, contributes q(e)/ppair(e)q(e)/p_{\mathrm{pair}}(e) to the rate second moment. The two consistent directions instantiate this as

full (q=p):wpair=pppair,wsamp=1;central (q=p2):wpair=p2ppair,wsamp=ppairp. \text{full } (q=p): \quad w^{\mathrm{pair}} = \frac{p}{p_{\mathrm{pair}}}, \quad w^{\mathrm{samp}} = 1; \qquad \text{central } (q=p^2): \quad w^{\mathrm{pair}} = \frac{p^2}{p_{\mathrm{pair}}}, \quad w^{\mathrm{samp}} = \frac{p_{\mathrm{pair}}}{p}.

(1)

The writeup never estimates ppairp_{\mathrm{pair}}. It substitutes the §A.5 identity ppair=p̂2p_{\mathrm{pair}} = \hat p^2, which collapses (1) to the implemented weights:

full:wpair=1p̂,wsamp=1;central:wpair=1,wsamp=p̂. \text{full}: \quad w^{\mathrm{pair}} = \frac{1}{\hat p}, \quad w^{\mathrm{samp}} = 1; \qquad \text{central}: \quad w^{\mathrm{pair}} = 1, \quad w^{\mathrm{samp}} = \hat p.

(2)

Note the asymmetry that answers “why would central’s weights change?”: under ppair=p̂2p_{\mathrm{pair}}=\hat p^2, central’s close-pair weight is exactly 11 — and it stays 11 under a direct estimate, because central’s defining target is the close-pair distribution, so its second moment never needs reweighting. What the p2p^2 assumption controls for central is the per-sample weight wsampw^{\mathrm{samp}} that pulls CtotalC_{\mathrm{total}}, CpsthC_{\mathrm{psth}} and the mean in to the close-pair distribution. For full it is the close-pair weight wpairw^{\mathrm{pair}}. Each direction therefore probes one factor of ppairp_{\mathrm{pair}}.

The §A.5 identity is exact for single-bin close pairs as Δe→0\Delta e\to0. The production estimator (§4.4) violates both premises: a finite threshold ε=0.05\varepsilon=0.05, and a close-pair filter that operates on the whole 12-bin RMS trajectory while the density is built on the reduced points ρi\rho_i. There is no theorem that ppair=p̂2p_{\mathrm{pair}}=\hat p^2 survives.

2. The 'direct' estimator

closepair_density='direct' fits a second KDE p̂pair\hat p_{\mathrm{pair}} on the realized close-pair representative-midpoints {12(ρi+ρj)}\{\tfrac12(\rho_i+\rho_j)\} and substitutes it for p̂2\hat p^2 in (1): full’s close-pair weight becomes p̂/p̂pair\hat p/\hat p_{\mathrm{pair}}, central’s per-sample weight becomes p̂pair/p̂\hat p_{\mathrm{pair}}/\hat p. Because every estimator is self-normalized, the unknown p2p^2 normalizer cancels and only the shapes of p̂\hat p and p̂pair\hat p_{\mathrm{pair}} matter. When p̂pair=p̂2\hat p_{\mathrm{pair}}=\hat p^2 the weights reduce to (2) exactly, so 'direct' ≡\equiv 'squared' wherever the identity holds.

Validation (TDD). Three tests in test_estimators.py pin this. In the single-bin regime and the zero-drift trajectory limit — where ppair=p2p_{\mathrm{pair}}=p^2 is provable — 'direct' recovers the flat-mask closed form 1−αp1-\alpha^p and agrees with 'squared' for Direction 1 (test_direct_closepair_density_*, test_trajectory_direct_closepair_density_*); and an unqualified call is confirmed to equal 'direct' (test_closepair_density_default_is_direct).

3. How far is p̂pair\hat p_{\mathrm{pair}} from p̂2\hat p^2?

For an isotropic Gaussian p=𝒩(0,σe2I)p=\mathcal N(0,\sigma_e^2 I) the §A.5 close-pair density is exactly 𝒩(0,σe22I)\mathcal N(0,\tfrac{\sigma_e^2}{2}I) — variance halved — so trcov(ρmid)/trcov(ρ)=0.5\operatorname{tr}\operatorname{cov}(\rho_{\mathrm{mid}})/\operatorname{tr} \operatorname{cov}(\rho)=0.5. On the real sessions the population median is 0.420.42 (range 0.230.23–0.850.85 across sessions), with KL(p̂pair∥p̂2)\mathrm{KL}(\hat p_{\mathrm{pair}}\,\|\,\hat p^2) median 0.160.16 (max 0.420.42). The departure is not noise: the real close-pair density is systematically more concentrated than p2p^2 (Fig. A, B). Two mechanisms push it there — real fixation densities are leptokurtic (a sharp central fixation peak plus a microsaccade-driven tail), and the whole-window RMS-trajectory match keeps the most stable, most central fixations — both of which over-represent the centre relative to the Gaussian p2p^2 prediction. The wide across-session range tracks cell yield: the high-yield Allen sessions sit at 0.280.28–0.380.38 and dominate the cell-weighted population, while the sparser Logan sessions scatter above 0.50.5.

  • naive and Fano are invariant — a built-in control. The naive estimator uses no importance weights, so it cannot depend on the close-pair-density estimate, and indeed it does not move at all. The corrected Fano factor (a count-per-mean dominated by CtotalC_{\mathrm{total}}) shifts by −0.001-0.001.

  • Direction 1 (full, the reported number) is robust. The population median rises by +0.017+0.017 and the per-cell median shift is +0.001+0.001 (per-cell |⋅||\cdot| median 0.0350.035, p90 0.2240.224; Fig. C). The §4.5 headline — “matching to the actual viewing distribution leaves the reported 1−α1-\alpha essentially unchanged” — survives the replacement of p̂2\hat p^2 by the directly-estimated p̂pair\hat p_{\mathrm{pair}}.

  • Direction 2 (central) drops by −0.060-0.060 (per-cell median −0.037-0.037, |⋅||\cdot| median 0.0570.057, p90 0.1860.186; Fig. D). This is exactly where ppairp_{\mathrm{pair}} acts on central — the per-sample reweight — and the sign follows the variance ratio: a more-central close-pair density pulls the central decomposition further toward the fixation peak, where the rate is least eye-modulated, lowering 1−α1-\alpha.

  • The gap widens, 0.119→0.1830.119\to0.183. full rises and central falls, so the full-vs-central gap — the fixation-scale spatial-structure measure of §4.3 — grows. Under 'direct' it is a cleaner measure: both ends are now consistent over their true sampling distributions rather than over an idealized p2p^2.

5. Bottom line

The squared-marginal shortcut ppair=p̂2p_{\mathrm{pair}}=\hat p^2 is measurably imperfect in the production trajectory-mode setting — real close pairs are more central than p2p^2 by a population-median variance ratio of 0.420.42 vs 0.50.5. Estimating the close-pair density directly removes the assumption. The reported Direction 1 1−α1-\alpha barely moves (+0.017+0.017 at the median, +0.001+0.001 per cell), so the main-writeup conclusions are now robust to it by measurement, not by appeal to the Δe→0\Delta e\to0 identity. The more distribution-sensitive quantities — Direction 2 and the full-vs-central gap — shift in the predicted direction, and 'direct' is the more principled estimator for them because it targets the close-pair distribution the data actually sample. On this basis 'direct' is now the estimator default and the main writeup reports it throughout; 'squared' is retained for the closed-form synthetic validation, where the ppair=p2p_{\mathrm{pair}}=p^2 identity holds exactly.
